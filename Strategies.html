Strategies To
Be Safe

There are a variety of ways one can be safe around drinking or drinkers. These help to promote an individual's health and safety.

1. Reporting Suspicious Activity

If you see a person whom you presume to be drunk, reporting them to local authorities helps you and others to stay safe. This prevents the person from causing any trouble, decreasing the chance that they might get hurt, or hurt other people. In New South Wales, the appropriate service to report suspicious activity to is Crime Stoppers.

2. Avoiding Alcohol

Avoiding scenarios and events where alcohol consumption takes place, such as parties or clubs, helps you to stay safe around alcohol. Not only does this prevent drunkeness but it also allows you to avoid people who can have a negative influence on you, which prevents issues such as peer pressure. This helps to limit your alcohol consumption and keep you healthy.

3. Avoiding Driving

If you have gotten drunk, then avoid driving at all costs. Drunk driving is one of the most common causes of car accidents and road deaths in the world. Drunk drivers may be persecuted under road laws. Instead, call a taxi or any other service or person to take you home. By avoiding drunk driving, you prevent any accidents, legal persecutions or financial losses.
